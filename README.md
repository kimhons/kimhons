## About Me

I'm a **Radiation Oncology Physicist** who traded dosimetry calculations for neural network architectures -- and somehow ended up building both. My career started in the precision world of medical physics, where fractions of a millimeter determine patient outcomes. That obsession with precision followed me into software engineering, where I now architect autonomous AI agent systems, full-stack products, and developer tools across **60+ repositories** in **10 programming languages**.

I'm the **Founder of [AlienNova](https://github.com/AllienNova)**, a bestselling author, an entrepreneur, and -- most importantly -- a father. Every project I ship is guided by a simple question: *does this make someone's life measurably better?*

<details>
<summary><b>My Journey</b></summary>
<br/>

From calibrating linear accelerators to calibrating large language models -- my path has been anything but linear. I earned my stripes in **Radiation Oncology Physics**, spending years ensuring cancer patients received precisely the right dose of radiation at precisely the right location. That discipline -- measure twice, compute thrice, verify everything -- became the foundation of my engineering philosophy.

The transition to software happened organically. I started writing Python scripts to automate treatment planning workflows, then built full applications for clinical teams, then realized I was spending more time coding than in the clinic. When LLMs arrived, I saw the same pattern I had witnessed in radiation therapy: a transformational technology that needed careful, precise engineering to deliver its potential safely.

Today I build at the intersection of AI, health, and developer experience. My work spans from **autonomous agent frameworks** like Lumina AI and Prometheus, to **consumer products** like KetoWell and Harmony Care, to **developer tools** like Claude Code Pro and VoiceCode. Every project carries the same DNA: rigorous architecture, comprehensive testing, and relentless focus on the end user.

I write books because teaching forces clarity. I build in public because accountability accelerates quality. I ship fast because the problems I'm solving can't wait.

</details>
